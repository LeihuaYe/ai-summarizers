### Google I/O 2026: Gemini 3.5 Flash + Antigravity 2.0
**May 19–20 · Google**

Google's biggest I/O AI push yet. ### Anthropic: Claude Mythos Preview (Project Glasswing)
**Early May · Anthropic**

Anthropic's unreleased frontier model **Claude Mythos Preview** is the week's most consequential announcement. In its first month deployed internally via **Project Glasswing** — a consortium of 50+ tech partners including Microsoft, Apple, Google, and Cloudflare — Mythos autonomously discovered over **10,000 high- and critical-severity zero-day vulnerabilities** across major operating systems, browsers, and critical infrastructure. It fully autonomously identified and exploited a 17-year-old RCE vulnerability in FreeBSD. Due to severe dual-use risk, Anthropic has withheld the model from public release, restricting it to defensive consortium members. The disclosure directly triggered the White House's push toward formal pre-release AI security reviews.

## 3. Notable AI Evaluation Research

### Apollo Research: Shift to "Science of Scheming"
Apollo Research's **May 2026 update** marks a strategic pivot: rather than running point evals on today's models, the team is now focused on **understanding the underlying science of whether and how scheming will arise in future systems** — studying how scaling trends (increased situational awareness, RL on longer-horizon tasks) shape model behavior. Key operational developments: (1) a new **monitoring team** building detectors for coding agent failure modes ranging from secrets leakage to oversight subversion; (2) a new **DC office opening in June** to work on policy implications around AI handoff and loss of control; (3) anticipated major focus on **automated AI R&D** implications through Q4 2026.

## 4. Key Industry Developments: Safety, Policy & Infrastructure

### US Government Mandates Pre-Deployment AI Testing
**May 5 · Commerce Department**

The US Commerce Department (NIST's Center for AI Standards and Innovation) announced that **Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI** have signed agreements to provide unreleased model versions for pre-release government security evaluation — joining OpenAI and Anthropic, which had existing arrangements. The evaluations assess national security domains including cybersecurity, biosecurity, and chemical weapons potential, often on versions with reduced safety guardrails. The center has completed **40+ model evaluations** to date. A potential executive order that could formalize and mandate these reviews (potentially with NSA involvement) is under active consideration, with the Trump administration citing the Anthropic Mythos disclosure as the proximate trigger.

## This Week's Highlight

**Anthropic Mythos + US Government Pre-Deployment Testing** is the most consequential development of the week for AI practitioners and policy watchers alike. A single model — withheld from public release — autonomously found more zero-day vulnerabilities in a month than most security firms find in years. That disclosure directly catalyzed a US government policy shift, pulling in Google, Microsoft, and xAI to a pre-release evaluation regime with potential executive order follow-through. The question of *who evaluates frontier models before deployment, and with what authority*, just became live policy, not academic.
