## This Week's Highlight
**LLM Judges Are Biased AND Unreliable as Optimization Signals**
Two back-to-back arXiv papers (July 12–13) reveal compounding limitations in LLM-as-judge evaluation: judges exhibit systematic positive bias without reference answers, and strong judging ability doesn't translate to reliable reward signals in closed-loop training. For practitioners using LLM-as-judge for model selection, A/B testing, or RLHF reward modeling, this means reference-free evaluation may substantially overestimate model quality — and that scoring ability is a poor proxy for training utility.
