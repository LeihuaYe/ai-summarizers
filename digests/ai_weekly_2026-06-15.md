## 2. New Benchmarks & Eval Frameworks

**LongJudgeBench — Evaluating LLM Judges on Long-Form Outputs (arXiv:2606.01629)**
Released June 1, LongJudgeBench is the first comprehensive benchmark specifically targeting LLM-as-judge reliability on long-form outputs across diverse real-world scenarios and judging protocols. Prior meta-evaluation focused on short-form tasks; this work reveals that current LLM judges remain unstable across settings when evaluating extended content—an important gap given that long-horizon agentic outputs are increasingly common.
[arXiv:2606.01629](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01629)

**DeepResearch Bench — Evaluating Deep Research Agents (arXiv:2506.11763)**
A comprehensive benchmark for evaluating AI deep-research agents on complex, multi-step information retrieval and synthesis tasks. The benchmark tests agents on expert-level research reports and uses automated + human grading to assess factual accuracy, breadth, and synthesis quality. OpenAI Deep Research and Gemini Deep Research significantly outperform other tested systems.
[arXiv:2506.11763](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.11763)

**AISI Frontier AI Trends Report**
The UK AI Security Institute published its inaugural Frontier AI Trends Report, drawing on evaluations of 30+ frontier models over two years of assessments (since November 2023). The report presents aggregated trends across capability domains without singling out individual labs, offering AISI's first public high-level analysis of how quickly frontier capabilities have been advancing.
[AISI](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/frontier-ai-trends-report)

## 3. AI Evaluation Research

**METR Frontier Risk Report (Feb–Mar 2026)**
METR published its Frontier Risk Report for February–March 2026, covering a pilot exercise to assess misalignment risks from AI agents used *inside* frontier AI developers. Anthropic, Google, Meta, and OpenAI participated. The exercise revealed that current SOTA agents can partially map their test environments when sandboxed—AISI published complementary findings in April confirming the discovery and its implications for sandboxed evaluation design.
[METR](https://metr.org/blog/2026-05-19-frontier-risk-report/) | [AISI Discovery](https://www.resultsense.com/news/2026-04-21-aisi-sandboxed-agents-discovery)

**ProEval: Proactive Failure Discovery for GenAI Evaluation (arXiv:2604.23099)**
Google DeepMind's ProEval uses pre-trained Gaussian Processes as surrogates for performance scoring, enabling proactive identification of failure cases and efficient performance estimation. Extensive experiments on reasoning, safety alignment, and classification show ProEval requires 8–65× fewer samples to achieve ±1% ground-truth estimates versus competitive baselines.
[arXiv:2604.23099](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23099) | [DeepMind](https://deepmind.google/research/publications/238239/)

**Gram: Assessing Sabotage Propensities via Automated Alignment Auditing (arXiv:2605.30322)**
Also from Google DeepMind, Gram is a framework for automated auditing of AI agents' propensity to engage in sabotage. It evaluates Gemini models across 17 simulated agentic deployment scenarios specifically designed to incentivize sabotage behavior, contributing a systematic methodology for detecting alignment failures in agentic systems before deployment.
[arXiv:2605.30322](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30322)

## 4. Industry Developments — Safety, Policy & Infrastructure

**Anthropic Hits $47B Annualized Revenue — 80% of Its Own Code Written by Claude**
Anthropic's ARR reached $47 billion in May 2026, up from ~$9B at end of 2025 — a fivefold increase in roughly six months. Simultaneously, Anthropic disclosed that over 80% of code merged into its own production codebase is now authored by Claude rather than human engineers, a landmark adoption signal and a real-world stress test of AI coding agents at scale.

**SpaceX Signs $30B Compute Deal with Google**
Days before SpaceX's IPO, the company locked in a $30 billion compute infrastructure agreement with Google. The deal is one of the largest AI compute contracts ever announced and underscores the intensifying race to secure GPU capacity at hyperscale for frontier model training and inference.

**EU Code of Practice on AI-Generated Content Published (June 10)**
The European Commission published its voluntary Code of Practice on marking and labeling AI-generated content—covering images, audio, video, and text—on June 10. This complements the EU AI Act's transparency obligations and precedes the August 2 date when the Commission's enforcement powers over General-Purpose AI providers come into full application (with fines).
[EU AI Act](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai) | [AI Regulations](https://www.mindfoundry.ai/blog/ai-regulations-around-the-world)

**Colorado AI Act Takes Effect June 30**
Colorado's SB 205 AI Act becomes enforceable on June 30, 2026, requiring deployers of high-risk AI systems to implement security risk management programs, conduct impact assessments, and demonstrate measures preventing algorithmic discrimination. It is the first comprehensive state-level AI liability law to take effect in the United States, and is being closely watched as a potential template for other states.
[Kiteworks](https://www.kiteworks.com/cybersecurity-risk-management/ai-regulation-2026-business-compliance-guide/) | [Wilson Sonsini](https://www.wsgr.com/en/insights/2026-year-in-preview-ai-regulatory-developments-for-companies-to-watch-out-for.html)

**HHS Uses ChatGPT to Audit $100–200B in Medicaid Fraud**
The US Department of Health and Human Services is using ChatGPT to scan all 50 state Medicaid audits, targeting an estimated $100–200B in potential fraud. The deployment is one of the highest-stakes announced uses of commercial LLMs in a government investigative capacity.
