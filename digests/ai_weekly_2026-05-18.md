## AI Evaluation

- **AISI: Autonomous AI Cyber Capability Doubling Every 4.7 Months** — Help Net Security / ResultSense | https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/05/14/ai-cyber-models-capability-projections/
  > The UK AI Security Institute published a new analysis on May 14 finding that frontier models' 80%-reliability cyber time horizon has doubled every 4.7 months since reasoning models emerged in late 2024 — roughly half the 8-month estimate from AISI's November 2025 report. In the latest round of evaluations, Claude Mythos Preview became the first model to complete two high-difficulty simulated cyber ranges: "The Last Ones," a 32-step corporate network attack (6/10 attempts), and the previously unsolved "Cooling Tower," a 7-step industrial-control-system attack (3/10 attempts). AISI noted that newer models are consistently surpassing earlier capability projections, invalidating earlier forecasts.

- **Terminal-Bench 2.0 and ResearchGym Formalize Agent Evaluation at ICLR 2026** — Snorkel AI / Stanford / Laude Institute | https://decodethefuture.org/en/ai-agent-benchmarks-2026/
  > Two new evaluation frameworks presented at ICLR 2026 push agent evals toward harder, more realistic task distributions. Terminal-Bench 2.0 (Snorkel AI, Stanford, Laude Institute) measures agent performance on realistic CLI and terminal tasks that require multi-step planning without scaffolding; ResearchGym makes agents run actual ML research experiments drawn from ACL, ICLR, and ICML papers across five task types and 39 sub-tasks. Both adopt Inspect, the UK AISI's open-source evaluation framework now used as the cross-organization standard by METR, Apollo Research, and both AI Safety Institutes.

- **Epistematics: Auditing Whether Benchmarks Actually Discriminate What They Claim** — arXiv (see Notable Papers)
  > A new methodology paper introduces Epistematics, a framework for deriving evaluation criteria directly from a capability claim and auditing whether a proposed benchmark can distinguish the claimed capability from proxy behaviors. The paper provides a failure-mode taxonomy and benchmark-design criteria, addressing the structural gap identified in recent bibliometric audits showing that many published evaluations test model-level proxies rather than deployment-relevant capabilities.

- **CHAL: Multi-Agent Dialectic Framework for Transparent, Auditable Reasoning** — arXiv:2605.12718 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12718
  > Published May 12, CHAL (Council of Hierarchical Agentic Language) treats defeasible argumentation as an engine for belief optimization across a committee of specialized agents. The system produces auditable belief artifacts — a chain of contestable reasoning steps with versioned defeaters — that the authors propose as a foundation for alignment evaluation suites focused on transparent reasoning and value consistency under challenge.

- **Deep Reasoning via Structured Meta-Cognition in General-Purpose Agents** — arXiv:2605.11388 | https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11388
  > Published May 12, this paper introduces an inference-time approach that constructs task-specific reasoning scaffolds through structured meta-cognition, representing meta-reasoning as executable decompositions over associative inference, formal computation, and recursive subproblem solving. Tested on frontier-class general-purpose agents, it improves reliability on hard reasoning tasks without additional fine-tuning — a practically deployable enhancement for production agents.

## This Week's Highlight

**Cyber Capability Acceleration Is Now a Policy Emergency**

The defining development of the past seven days is the convergence of two previously separate storylines into a single inflection point. The UK AISI's May 14 finding — that frontier model cyber capability is doubling every 4.7 months — arrived in the same week that the White House confirmed it is drafting an executive order specifically in response to Claude Mythos demonstrating autonomous multi-step network compromise. These are no longer abstract risk-model projections: they are measurements from live frontier models showing up in real policy text.

What makes the AISI number striking is its acceleration. Eight months was the doubling estimate in November 2025; 4.7 months is the May 2026 number. That compression means the window between "capability demonstrated in evaluation" and "capability accessible to adversaries via model APIs" is shrinking faster than institutional response times. The FDA-style vetting process the NEC director invoked this week was designed for timescales measured in years. The capability curve being measured by AISI and METR is currently on a timescale measured in months.

For practitioners: the NIST pre-deployment testing agreements now covering all five major labs represent the most concrete governance infrastructure the U.S. has, but the voluntary nature and self-certification model are already under pressure from the pace of the numbers above. The next three months — before any executive order could take effect and with Grok 5 and Meta Avocado still on the horizon — will be the clearest test of whether voluntary agreements hold when capability growth outstrips prior commitments.
