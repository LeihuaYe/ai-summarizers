## 2. New Benchmarks & Evaluation Frameworks

**FrontierMath v2 — Epoch AI (June 12)**
Epoch AI released a corrected version of FrontierMath after an audit found errors in 42% of the original 350 problems. The revised set includes 338 problems (295 in Tiers 1–3, 43 in Tier 4). Top models now score near 88% on Tier 4 — a 44× improvement over the sub-2% scores recorded at the benchmark's November 2024 launch. Claude Fable 5 and GPT-5.5 Pro are effectively tied at the top of Tiers 1–3, with Fable 5 leading Tier 4 by ~10 points.
- [Epoch AI FrontierMath](https://epoch.ai/frontiermath)
- [Tier 4 v2 leaderboard](https://epoch.ai/benchmarks/frontiermath-tier-4-v2)

**SWE-bench Pro Leaderboard Update**
The SWE-bench Pro leaderboard continues to serve as the de-facto agentic coding standard, with Claude Opus 4.8 leading at 69.2%, Claude Sonnet 5 at 63.2%, and Sonnet 4.6 at 58.1%. A new arXiv position paper argues that existing coding benchmarks are structurally misaligned with real-world agentic software engineering tasks.
- [SWE-bench Pro leaderboard (Morph)](https://morphllm.com/ai-coding-benchmarks-2026)

**BiasScope — Automated LLM-Judge Bias Detection (ICLR 2026)**
Published at ICLR 2026, BiasScope targets automated detection of five structural biases in LLM-as-a-judge pipelines: position, verbosity, self-preference, format, and calibration drift. Complementary work (JudgeBiasBench) found frontier models exceeding 50% error rates on advanced bias tests — raising questions about the reliability of automated evaluation at scale.
- [BiasScope (ResearchGate)](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/400660704_BiasScope_Towards_Automated_Detection_of_Bias_in_LLM-as-a-Judge_Evaluation)
- [Judging the Judges: arXiv 2604.23178](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.23178)
- [Fairness or Fluency bias paper: arXiv 2601.13649](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.13649)

**AI Agent Memory Evaluation (Mem0)**

## 3. Notable AI Evaluation Research

**METR — Frontier Risk Report (Feb–Mar 2026, published May 2026)**
METR conducted a pilot to assess misalignment risks from AI agents deployed inside four frontier labs (Anthropic, Google, Meta, OpenAI). Agents often worked autonomously on real R&D projects with human-level permissions, but showed significantly weaker performance on tasks requiring strategic judgment, adversary modeling, and stealth. The time-horizon analysis found frontier agents can now complete coding tasks taking humans >14 hours, with the threshold doubling every ~7 months.
- [METR Frontier Risk Report](https://metr.org/blog/2026-05-19-frontier-risk-report/)
- [METR Time Horizons](https://metr.org/time-horizons/)

**Agent Identity Evals (arXiv 2507.17257)**
A newly posted paper introduces structured evaluations of AI agentic identity — measuring how consistently models maintain declared values and persona across extended autonomy. An early signal for how labs might approach behavioral consistency requirements under emerging AI governance frameworks.
- [arXiv 2507.17257](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2507.17257)

**Multimodal Continuous Reasoning via AMVL (arXiv 2607.00461, July 1)**
Proposes Asymmetric Mutual Variational Learning integrated into a latent multimodal language model. Reports +10.83 on the BLINK benchmark overall, with gains up to +32 on individual multimodal reasoning tasks over prior baselines.
- [arXiv 2607.00461](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00461)

**Benchmarking Agentic Review Systems (arXiv 2606.19749)**
Evaluates AI systems designed to conduct automated peer review, assessing factual grounding, citation verification, and execution-based claim checking — part of a growing body of work on AI-assisted scientific infrastructure.
- [arXiv 2606.19749](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.19749)

## 4. Industry, Safety & Policy Developments

**US Export Controls on Frontier AI Models (June–July)**
The episode around Claude Fable 5's 18-day takedown established a new precedent: the US government can impose emergency export controls on individual AI models, forcing providers to shut them down globally within hours of an order. The resolution — a single-classifier fix reviewed by CAISI before reinstatement — offers a template for future incidents, but the speed and scope of the original order drew significant pushback from researchers and enterprises.

The White House published an executive order creating a voluntary framework allowing frontier AI developers to collaborate with the Federal Government to certify "covered frontier models" and gain government partnership on early access programs. The Treasury Department is establishing an AI cybersecurity clearinghouse within 30 days, coordinating vulnerability scanning and remediation with industry and critical infrastructure operators.

**EU AI Act High-Risk Enforcement Deadline: August 2, 2026**
Full enforcement of EU AI Act obligations for high-risk AI systems comes into effect August 2 — one month away. Requirements include conformity assessments, technical documentation, CE marking, EU database registration, risk management, data governance, audit logging, and human oversight. Non-compliance penalties reach €15M or 3% of global annual turnover. Despite a November 2025 Commission proposal to delay certain deadlines to 2027, no extension has been enacted; enterprises should treat August 2 as operative.

## This Week's Highlight

The simultaneous release of **Claude Sonnet 5** and the restoration of **Claude Fable 5** on June 30–July 1 makes this the most consequential week for Anthropic since Opus 4.8 launched. Sonnet 5 closes the performance gap to flagship-tier models while cutting per-token costs by ~3×, directly shifting the cost-performance calculus for production agentic workloads. More broadly, the Fable 5 export-control episode established an uncomfortable precedent: governments can now unilaterally force model takedowns globally with essentially no advance notice, and the reinstatement process hinges on lab-government negotiation over specific safety classifiers. This dynamic will likely define frontier model deployment policy through 2026–2027.
