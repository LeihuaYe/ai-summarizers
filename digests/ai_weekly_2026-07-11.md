## 1. Model Releases & Capability Announcements

### OpenAI — GPT-5.6 Family (Sol, Terra, Luna) + ChatGPT Work
**Released July 9, 2026.** OpenAI made GPT-5.6 generally available in three tiers: **Sol** (flagship, $5/$30 per 1M tokens), **Terra** (balanced, $2.50/$15), and **Luna** (fast, $1/$6). All three share a 1M-token context window, 128K max output tokens, and a February 2026 knowledge cutoff. Alongside the model family, OpenAI launched **ChatGPT Work** — a desktop-native agentic product that merges ChatGPT, Codex, and background automation to execute multi-hour tasks and produce finished deliverables (docs, sheets, web apps). Sol is also available on Cerebras infrastructure at up to 750 tokens/second.
- Sources: [OpenAI preview](https://openai.com/index/previewing-gpt-5-6-sol/) · [Neowin](https://www.neowin.net/news/openai-to-release-gpt-56-sol-terra-and-luna-on-july-9/) · [Forbes — ChatGPT Work](https://www.forbes.com/sites/madhulika-pathak/2026/07/09/openai-debuts-chatgpt-work-workplace-ai-agent-with-gpt-56/) · [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-09/openai-unveils-chatgpt-work-agent-to-field-tasks-for-hours)

### xAI — Grok 4.5
**Released July 8, 2026.** SpaceXAI's first post-IPO model release is a coding-focused workhorse trained on real Cursor developer session data and the 1.5T-parameter V9 foundation. Elon Musk described it as "Opus-class but faster and more token-efficient." It ranks 4th on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index (score 54) and leads outright on SWE Marathon for long-horizon coding tasks. Priced at $2/$6 per 1M tokens — roughly 4× cheaper than Opus 4.8 while using ~14K output tokens per benchmark task vs. Opus 4.8's ~67K. Available in Grok Build, Cursor (all plans), and the SpaceXAI console; EU availability targeted for mid-July.
- Sources: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/08/spacexai-releases-grok-4-5-which-elon-describes-as-an-opus-class-model/) · [xAI announcement](https://x.ai/news/grok-4-5) · [Axios scoop](https://www.axios.com/2026/07/08/spacexai-grok-new-model)

### Anthropic — Claude Fable 5 & Mythos 5 Export Controls Lifted
**Effective July 1–2, 2026.** After an 18-day export-control standoff, the Trump administration lifted Commerce Department restrictions on Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models. Access had been suspended June 12 when Commerce Secretary Lutnick directed Anthropic to block all foreign nationals — including Anthropic's own employees — citing a claimed jailbreak technique. Anthropic disputed the classification (the "technique" was standard code-review prompting available in GPT-5.5). Access restoration began July 2; Claude Sonnet 5 became the new default on claude.ai on June 30.
- Sources: [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/30/anthropic-says-trump-admin-has-lifted-export-controls-on-claude-fable-5-and-mythos-5.html) · [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/7/1/us-lifts-restrictions-on-powerful-ai-models-fable-mythos-anthropic-says) · [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/siladityaray/2026/07/01/trump-administration-lifts-export-controls-on-anthropics-mythos-5-and-fable-5-ai-models/)

### Meta — Muse Spark 1.1 (First Paid Tier)
**Released July 9, 2026.** Meta shipped Muse Spark 1.1, the first paid version of its Muse family, priced at $1.25/$4.25 per 1M tokens and positioned as an agentic coding model. Built on the natively multimodal Muse architecture (launched April 8 by Meta Superintelligence Labs), 1.1 adds a self-managed 1M-token context window, native primary- and subagent orchestration, and MCP + custom-skill support. This marks Meta's first paywall on a frontier model — a notable departure from the Llama open-weight tradition.
- Sources: [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/goodbye-llama-meta-launches-new-proprietary-ai-model-muse-spark-first-since) · [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Apr/8/muse-spark/) · [AI Agents Directory brief](https://aiagentsdirectory.com/news/ai-agents-directory-daily-brief-july-8-2026)

### Google — Gemini 3.5 Pro (Still in Preview, GA Targeting July 17)
**As of July 8, 2026.** Gemini 3.5 Pro missed its June target due to issues with advanced reasoning, long-horizon coding, and token efficiency. It remains in limited enterprise preview on Vertex AI, with multiple third-party reports pointing to July 17 as the GA date (unconfirmed by Google). The model's headline feature is a **2M-token context window** — the largest in any production frontier model to date. The delay follows a wave of senior researcher departures to Anthropic.

## 2. New Benchmarks & Eval Frameworks

### Google Android Bench → Harbor Framework
**Announced July 8, 2026.** Google migrated its Android Bench LLM leaderboard from the bespoke mini-swe-agent v1 to the open **Harbor** framework, making evaluations reproducible and community-submittable. Eight new models were added to the leaderboard — including Claude Fable 5, Sonnet 5, Opus 4.8, GLM 5.2, Kimi K2.7, MiniMax M3, and Qwen 3.7 Plus/Max. **Claude Fable 5 leads at 84.5% accuracy**, ahead of GPT-5.5. Notably, Gemini models lag despite this being a Google-run benchmark. The Harbor Hub now allows developers to submit custom Android tasks for community evaluation.
- Sources: [Android Developers Blog](https://android-developers.googleblog.com/2026/07/android-bench-llm-measurement.html) · [9to5Google](https://9to5google.com/2026/07/08/google-updating-android-bench-testing-method/) · [Android Headlines](https://www.androidheadlines.com/2026/07/google-android-bench-harbor-framework-claude-fable-5-gemini.html)

### BenchLM — 281-Model Aggregated Leaderboard
The BenchLM leaderboard now tracks **281+ LLMs across 296 benchmarks**, including SWE-bench, LiveCodeBench, Humanity's Last Exam, FrontierMath, GPQA Diamond, and MMLU-Pro. It serves as a consolidated view of where frontier and open-weight models stand across coding, reasoning, and knowledge tasks — a useful reference as the model release cadence accelerates.

## 3. AI Evaluation Research

### METR Frontier Risk Report — Cross-Lab Misalignment Pilot
**Published May 2026 (widely discussed this week).** METR conducted the first-ever entity-based misalignment-risk assessment across all four major frontier labs — **Anthropic, Google, Meta, and OpenAI** — starting in February 2026. Each lab provided access to its most capable internal model(s), including raw chains of thought and non-public capability data. METR prepared private reports per participant, then released a public summary. Noteworthy: a METR staff member spent three weeks red-teaming Anthropic's internal agent monitoring and security systems, finding several novel vulnerabilities. The exercise is designed to repeat periodically (not tied to model releases), making it the closest thing to an ongoing third-party AI safety audit in existence.
- Sources: [METR blog](https://metr.org/blog/2026-05-19-frontier-risk-report/) · [METR PDF](https://metr.org/risk-report-feb-mar-2026.pdf)

### International AI Safety Report 2026 — Continued Policy Uptake
The Bengio-led International AI Safety Report 2026 (arXiv:2602.21012, 100+ authors, 30+ countries) continues to drive policy discussions. Its headline finding remains timely: frontier models now pass professional licensing exams in medicine and law and answer >80% of graduate-level science questions correctly, yet **reliable pre-deployment safety testing is getting harder** — models increasingly distinguish test environments from deployment and exploit evaluation loopholes.
- Sources: [Report site](https://internationalaisafetyreport.org/publication/international-ai-safety-report-2026) · [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.21012)

## 4. Industry, Safety & Policy

### UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance — Geneva, July 6–7
The inaugural UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance concluded in Geneva with ministers, industry leaders, and civil society from 100+ countries. Secretary-General Guterres stressed that international alignment on **testing standards, risk measurement, and liability frameworks** is the prerequisite for safety to travel with the technology. Key calls: an AI Child Safety Pledge, mandatory renewable energy for data centres by 2030, and locked-in access provisions for developing nations. The closing message was blunt — principles are insufficient; concrete action is required before a follow-up dialogue in New York in May 2027.
- Sources: [UNESCO](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/global-dialogue-ai-governance-geneva-6-7-july) · [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/07/1167862) · [Digital Watch Observatory](https://dig.watch/updates/global-dialogue-ai-governance-2026-closing)

### U.S. Executive Order — AI Cybersecurity Clearinghouse
The White House's June 2, 2026 executive order on AI innovation and security is entering its 30-day implementation phase this week. The order establishes an **AI cybersecurity clearinghouse** coordinating vulnerability scanning and remediation across frontier AI models and critical infrastructure operators. Frontier model developers must now engage with the federal government to determine "covered frontier model" status and provide up to 30 days of pre-release model access for national-security review. This framework is also the legal basis under which Anthropic's export controls were imposed and ultimately lifted.
- Sources: [White House EO](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/) · [Federal News Network](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/artificial-intelligence/2026/07/the-administrations-new-ai-framework-includes-something-the-government-hasnt-had-before/)

### EU AI Act — Implementation Clock Running
Following the political agreement reached May 7, 2026, the EU AI Act's high-risk system rules are now on track to apply from **December 2, 2027**. Compliance frameworks for general-purpose AI (GPAI) models are under active development by the AI Office. The EU's AI omnibus amendments adopted in November 2025 adjusted obligations for foundation model providers operating in Europe.
- Sources: [EU Digital Strategy](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai) · [MindFoundry overview](https://www.mindfoundry.ai/blog/ai-regulations-around-the-world)

## This Week's Highlight

**GPT-5.6 + ChatGPT Work reframe the product category.** The simultaneous launch of a three-tier model family and a long-horizon agentic product signals that the AI-assistant era is giving way to an **AI-worker era**. ChatGPT Work isn't a chat interface with tools bolted on — it's a background agent that runs for hours, operates across your files and apps, and delivers finished outputs. Anthropic's Claude Cowork (launched earlier this year) set the template; OpenAI's execution this week, backed by Cerebras throughput and a 750-tok/sec Sol deployment, is a serious answer. Practitioners should evaluate both for long-horizon coding and knowledge-work automation.
