## AI Progress

- **OpenAI Releases GPT-5.5 — New Intelligence Crown** — OpenAI Blog | https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-5/
  > Released April 23–24, GPT-5.5 tops public leaderboards across agentic and knowledge-work benchmarks: 82.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0 (vs. Opus 4.7's 69.4%), 84.9% GDPval wins-or-ties, 78.7% OSWorld-Verified, and 35.4% FrontierMath Tier 4. Its defining feature is long-horizon autonomous execution — sustaining productive coding runs up to 13 hours without user intervention. It leads open models on SWE-Bench Pro at 58.6% (vs. 57.7% for GPT-5.4 xhigh and 53.4% for Claude Opus 4.6).

- **Grok 4.3 Beta: Native Video, Doc Generation, 2M-Token Context** — xAI Release Notes | https://grok.com/release-notes
  > xAI quietly shipped Grok 4.3 Beta on April 17 as an Early Access tier, adding native video input, in-chat slide decks, downloadable PDFs, and fully populated spreadsheets. The 2M-token context window and 16-agent Heavy system carry over from Grok 4.20, keeping it the largest context window among Western closed models. Access requires the SuperGrok Heavy plan at $300/month; broader rollout is estimated for late May.

- **GDPVal Cements Status as the Economic-Productivity Benchmark** — OpenAI / Artificial Analysis | https://openai.com/index/gdpval/
  > Covering 1,320 tasks across 44 knowledge-work occupations in 9 U.S. GDP sectors, GDPval saw heavy citation this week as GPT-5.5 (84.9%), Claude Opus 4.7 (80.3%), and Gemini 3.1 Pro (67.3%) posted public scores. The benchmark's framing — AI vs. industry experts with 14 years average experience on real deliverables — is shifting how labs communicate practical capability gains to enterprise buyers. Public leaderboard and gold subset of 220 tasks are open-source. (arXiv:2510.04374)

- **MLCommons Releases MLPerf Inference v6.0 With Text-to-Video and GPT-OSS 120B Tests** — MLCommons | https://mlcommons.org/2026/04/mlperf-inference-v6-0-results/
  > Released April 1 (slightly pre-window but first fully digested this week), MLPerf v6.0 adds five new or updated datacenter tests including text-to-video generation, a GPT-OSS 120B math/science/coding benchmark, and DLRMv3 (the first sequential recommendation test in the suite). 24 organizations submitted results; AMD, NVIDIA Blackwell Ultra, and Google all posted performance highlights.

- **"Intent Laundering": Safety Datasets Are Systematically Flawed** — arXiv:2602.16729 | https://arxiv.org/html/2602.16729v1
  > A February paper receiving significant practitioner attention this week demonstrates that AI safety datasets over-rely on unrealistic triggering cues, enabling "intent laundering" — rephrasing attacks that achieve 90–98%+ success rates against current safety training under black-box access. The finding has direct implications for how labs should design safety evals: the distribution of adversarial prompts in benchmarks diverges substantially from real-world attack distributions.
